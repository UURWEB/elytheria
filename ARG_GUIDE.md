# Elytheria Tourism ARG — Owner Guide

## Public premise

The Kingdom of Elytheria is a peaceful U.U.R. crown-state centered on Boletus. Queen Neon serves as ceremonial sovereign while the Mushroom Council and Boletus Commons conduct ordinary state and city government under Union law.

The old tourism site is maintained by well-meaning GardenNet volunteers. Neither Queen Neon nor the Elytherian government knows that an abandoned web-ring redirect has been repurposed.

## Hidden story

A genuine Astari network uses forgotten public sites as a quiet backend beneath the wider U.U.R. web. The Boletus site is one relay among radio archives, NCN mirrors, commercial directories, and old public-service pages.

The ridiculous Astari defacement on the old U.U.R. military page was not a real Astari operation. “Operator Sandglass” was a child in Central who found an old public webmaster password. Union authorities seized the credentials and handled the juvenile matter. The Astari network considers Sandglass an embarrassment and orders its operators not to contact them.

## Player path

1. On `queen.html`, the first letters of Queen Neon’s seven blessings spell **BENEATH**.
2. On `boletus.html`, the first letters of the Mushroom Walk spell **MUSHROOM**.
3. `webring.html` instructs players to put **THE** between the two messages.
4. A nearly invisible crimson star at the bottom of the web-ring page opens `garden/root.html`.
5. Root phrase: `BENEATH THE MUSHROOM`.
6. The route table in `garden/network.html` and `OPEN ROUTES` in the console spell **CRIMSON** with their first letters. The clue says to add the object every Astari knows.
7. Selection phrase: `CRIMSON STAR`.
8. In the console, enter `AUTH CRIMSON STAR`, then `ROUTE RECRUIT`.

## Central City Radio carrier meaning

The Astari network does not control Central City Radio and the station is not knowingly involved. A very low-bandwidth encoded subcarrier is hidden beneath the station’s ordinary broadcast carrier. It carries only timing pings and short route instructions, letting Astari listeners confirm that the quiet network is still alive. To normal listeners, Central City Radio sounds unchanged.
